The Heart of Our Kitchen

Chef Abdullah Al-Najdi

Master of Traditional Najdi Cuisine

Born and raised in the heart of Riyadh, Chef Abdullah's culinary journey began in his grandmother's kitchen, where the aromatic spices and traditional cooking methods of Najd captured his heart at a young age. With over 25 years of experience, he has dedicated his life to preserving and sharing the authentic flavors of Saudi Arabia's central region.

After formal culinary training in prestigious institutions, Chef Abdullah traveled across the Arabian Peninsula, studying regional variations of traditional dishes. However, his heart always remained with the simple, hearty meals of his childhood - the dishes that told stories of Bedouin heritage, family gatherings, and the rich culinary history of Najd.

My mission is to share the soul of Najdi cooking with the world. Every dish tells a story of our ancestors, our land, and our traditions. When you taste our food, you're not just eating - you're experiencing centuries of culinary heritage.

At Najd Kitchen, Chef Abdullah personally oversees the preparation of every meal, ensuring that each dish maintains the authenticity and heart of traditional home cooking. His signature touch can be tasted in the perfectly balanced spices of our Jareesh, the tender meat in our Qursan, and the flaky perfection of our Kleeja pastries.

Preserving Culinary Heritage

Chef Abdullah believes that food is more than sustenance - it's a living history. Each dish at Najd Kitchen represents a piece of Saudi cultural heritage, prepared with the same techniques and love that have been used for generations.

From selecting the finest local ingredients to using traditional cooking vessels, every aspect of our preparation honors the culinary traditions of central Arabia. Our recipes have been carefully documented and preserved, ensuring that future generations can continue to enjoy these authentic flavors.

  • All spices are sourced directly from local Saudi markets
  • Traditional cooking methods preserved and honored
  • Recipes passed down through four generations
  • Focus on seasonal and locally-sourced ingredients
